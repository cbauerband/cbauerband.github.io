About Me

I was born in New Jersey, though I spent much of my childhood in Kumba, Cameroon. I now reside in Lafayette, Indiana. The third culture childhood has affected many parts of my life. It changed how I view the world, how I approach different topics, and how I think globally. It's also led me to visit Honduras in the form of a mission trip, where I helped add a school house to an orphanage.

I enjoy art, hiking, video games, and have recently developed an interest in genealogy. I enjoy learning, both in school settings and with hands on work. I feel better when I'm doing something productive and learning something new, rather than being content with where I am at.

I am a current student at Eleven Fifty Coding Academy in the Web Development course. I am also enrolled at Ivy Tech Community College in Lafayette. I am hoping to be a Junior Web Developer.

With Eleven Fifty Coding Academy's education, I am familiar with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript languages.



More About Me

My family and I ended up at a small seminary in Kumba, Cameroon, when I was four years old. My father was a minister and teacher and my mother made efforts with the women of the villages to find them work. My family arranged for carvings, wall hangings, and drums made by the locals to be sold at a fair price every summer in the United States. It was a hard life for the people there, but there was much joy.

I was homeschooled by my mother, who taught me a great deal about English and Math, and significantly less about science and geography. Most of my learning during those years was very hands on. I helped count inventory for the church's auctions and document it, I helped with planning how many toothbrushes and toothpastes we needed to bring back to Cameroon at the end of each summer, and I read biblical passages at local weddings.

I credit a lot of who I am now to that childhood spent overseas. I am humbled by my experiences and I know not to take anything for granted. I learned the meaning of hard work and integrity as a child, and those lessons will always stick with me.

In 2011, I had the honor of going on a school mission trip to Honduras. The goal was simple: build a three room school house that would be used by the orphanage's children. It was a physically demanding job, and I learned much more about construction than I'd ever thought I'd know.

I helped teach the children some English and they taught me some Spanish in return. We played ball outside Casa Cielo and drew pictures in the dirt. They reminded me how to make cars out of cans and wires, something I had not done in quite a few years. It reminded me of my roots and strengthened my desire to be a helper.

Art has long been a part of my life. I've been scribbling on paper as long as I can remember. I'd illustrate little books that I had written. In my teenage years I expanded into ceramics, painting, and writing short stories. My favorite media to work with will always be clay, as there's something very satisfying about making something beautiful from it. I specialize in mold-based ceramics, where I use molds to shape plates or bowls and then add my own touches to them.
